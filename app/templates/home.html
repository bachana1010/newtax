types of taxes

Information on three main taxes

value added tax.

Value-added tax (VAT) is a consumption tax on goods and services that is levied at each stage of the supply chain where value is added,
from initial production to the point of sale.
amount of VAT the user pays is based on the cost of the product minus any costs of materials
in the product that have already been taxed at a previous stage. The VAT rate shall be 18%.

Property Tax

Property taxable property and land. The annual property tax
rate for an enterprise/organisation shall be determined in the maximum amount of 1% of the value of the taxable property.
the purposes of this paragraph, the value of taxable property shall be the average annual net book value

Profit Tax

The object of profit taxation of a resident enterprise shall be:
a) distributed profit;
b) costs incurred or other payments not related to economic activity;
c) free delivery of goods/services and/or transfer of funds;
d) representation expenses paid in excess of a limited amount determined under this Code.
The profit tax rate shall be 15%.
